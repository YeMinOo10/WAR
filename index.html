The Great Wars

World War-1

World War I, also known as the Great War, began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria.
His murder catapulted into a war across Europe that lasted until 1918.

Countries involved in WWW-1

Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria and the Ottoman Empire (the Central Powers) fought against
Great Britain, France, Russia, Italy, Romania, Canada, Japan and the United States (the Allied Powers).

World War-2

World War II, also called Second World War, conflict that involved
virtually every part of the world during the years 1939–45.

Countries involved in WWW-2

The principal belligerents were the Axis powers—Germany, Italy, and Japan—and the Allies—France,
Great Britain, the United States, the Soviet Union, and, to a lesser extent, China.

The Cold War

The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union and
their respective allies, the Western Bloc and the Eastern Bloc, which began following World War II.

Countries involved in The Cold War

North Atlantic Treaty Organization, Soviet Union, United States and Warsaw Treaty Organization.
